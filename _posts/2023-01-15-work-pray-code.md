Title: Work Pray Code: When Work Becomes Religion in Silicon Valley.

The title of the book summarizes the content well. Technology companies create a bubble by offering high salaries and great perks to their employees. They work long hours and endure immense pressure, without caring or having time to care about public affairs. The companies arrange spiritual and meditative activities, with religious undertones, solely to help employees improve their focus and mental state for work. The company has become the only place for these people to attain a sense of wholeness. Social, health, leisure activities, and a sense of belonging, all occur within the company.

A single mother changes jobs to a new company because the new company can help take care of her children and prepare dinner. This way, she can allocate more time to work, and her children receive good care and nutrition. A win-win situation. The power of capitalism. Techtopia.

The high cost of living in the Bay Area has pushed out workers from other industries. Local governments haven't received much tax revenue because of the tax avoidance strategies employed by large corporations. 

Constructive suggestion: Humen always have something to admire. seek wholeness beyond their jobs and in a broader space. Public participation requires many people to come together.
