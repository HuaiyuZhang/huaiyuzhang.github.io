The beginning of the book talks about the impact of stories on cognition. "Story" here is the narratives and interpretation of one's history, which in turn can affect the emotion and behavior. In cognitive behavioral theropy, there is a similar triangle: thoughts, emotions, and behavior. 

The author talks about a client who cares about others but ignores their own feelings. She sacrisfies a lot for others but does not receive anything similar back. She feels she provides no value to people and she is not worthy of kindness from others. When she was young, she always played the role of taking care of her younger sister and brothers. The only time she received positive attention was when she suppress her own needs and did what others needed of her. She got more confirmation that she needed to be needless in order to feel worthy.

**Comment**: The feedbacks, positive or negative, shape our psycological tendency and behavior. As parents, intentional and unintentional responses will lead our children to different paths. Is there any perfect path? Or the life is alway in a mess and we have to admit and accept it?

**Themes of stories**
- Inner critic
- Perfectionism
- Sense of not belonging (hiding parts of yourself)
- Difficulty in relationships 
- People pleasing (it seems to center others, but it is a way of feeling like we have control of how those people perceive us)
- Highligtend shame
- Self doubt
- Imposter Syndrome

Separate yourself from your story: give the story a name; view the story from someone else's perspective; no longer labeling yourself as the problem but naming the story as something you are experiencing.

**Four supportive mindsets**
- Mindfulness (Be aware of feelings)
- Curiosity (Other possibilities)
- Self-compassion (Love, forgive, and surrender)
- aligned action (Gentle discipline; take self-responsibility)
